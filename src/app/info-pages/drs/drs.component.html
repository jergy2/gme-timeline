DRS
Direct Registration System
Summary
  • Cede & Co., a private company and partner of the DTC, legally owns the large majority of all issued stocks in the United States
    • As of 1998, Cede & Co. owned 83% of all issued stocks in the United States
    • Since 1998, this ownership percentage has not been publicly released again
    • When shares of a company are purchased at a stock broker, the buyer becomes the beneficial owner of the shares, but the legal owner of those shares is Cede & Co.
    • Due to lack of transparency by Cede & Co. and the DTC, the number of share entitlements that exist among stock brokers and other market participants cannot be verified
  • DRS is an acronym for Direct Registration System
    • DRS is a mechanism that enables investors to remove shares from the DTC's ownership and registers them with the issuing company's transfer agent in the name of the investor, making them them the legal owner of the shares
    • Holding shares in DRS form is the only way to have genuine legal ownership of your shares
  • GameStop's transfer agent is Computershare
  • GME shareholders are challenging the status quo by directly registering billions of dollars worth of shares
    • Approximately 177,000 individuals have registered their shares of GME
    • Approximately 61.5 million shares, or 14.4% of all issued shares of GME are held in DRS form
    • Combined, this represents the single largest portion of GME ownership
    • No other publicly traded company in history has had a significant portion of its shares directly registered by a collective of thousands of individual household investors
Held by registered holders with Computershare: DRS versus DSPP

All shares held with GameStop's transfer agent Computershare are considered "registered", which means that the individual investor is listed by name on the issuer's (GameStop's) stock ledger (also referred to as the "register") and the investor is therefore known to the company.

GameStop reports this information in their 10-Q / 10-K filings.
For example, in the most recent 10-Q, June 11, 2024:

... approximately 74.6 million shares of our Class A common stock were held by registered holders with our transfer agent (or approximately 21% of our outstanding shares) as of June 5, 2024.

However, of registered shares, there is an important distinction between shares held in DRS form versus shares held in DSPP form.

  • DRS
    • The investor is registered by name as the owner of the shares in the main class on GameStop's stock ledger (or register)
    • Though the shares are accessed and managed in an account with Computershare, the shares are formally "held" directly by the shareholder without any intermediary
    • Holding shares in DRS form provides both ownership and possession of shares
  • DSPP
    • The direct stock purchase plan (DSPP) is a proprietary Computershare service
    • Shares in the plan are not held by the investor
      • 80% to 90% of the shares are held by Dingo & Co (Computershare's nominee)
      • 10% to 20% of the shares are held by Cede & Co for operational efficiency
    • The investor is registered by name as the owner of the shares in a subclass on the ledger
    • Holding shares in the plan provides ownership but not possession of the shares
